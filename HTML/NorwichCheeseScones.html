Norwich cheese scones

Ingredients

  • 450g self-raising flour
  • Pinch salt
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 6 tsp baking powder
  • 110g butter
  • 280g cheddar cheese (plus extra for topping the scones)
  • 200 ml milk, plus extra for glazing
  • 3 tbsp Coleman's mustard

  • Regular oven: 220 degrees, gas mark 7
  • Cooking Time: 10-15 minutes
  • Servings:12 scones

Method

  1. Leave the butter on the counter beforehand
  2. Put the dry ingredients into a bowl and blend
  3. Put the butter into the dry ingredients and start making fine breadcrumbs
  4. Add the grated cheese (keep back some for the of of the scones) and mustard and blend in.
  5. Pour in the milk a little at a time and mix until well combined and dough like.
  6. Put some flour on to the countertop and place the mix on the flour.
  7. Roll out the mixture with a floured rolling pin, until the mix is around 3cm high
  8. Cut out circles with a medium shaped cutter
  9. Put on a baking tray and brush with a little milk
  10. Add some grated cheese on each scone.
  11. Place in the middle of the oven for 10-15 minutes
  12. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

Notes:

  • No rolling pin? Use a strong round glass (be careful not to press to firmly and break the glass)
  • Run out of milk to put on top of the scones? Beat an egg and wash it across the scones instead.
  • No cutter to make round scones? You can use a round glass or mug
  • Most of the combining of ingredients can be done with clean hands
  • If you forget to leave the butter out beforehand, then you can warm the butter in the microwave until just soft (30 seconds).
  • If you aren't keen on dumplings then cheese scones make a nice alternative to accompany soups or stews
