Chocolatechip cookies

Ingredients

  • 250g plain flour
  • Â½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 150g butter
  • 210g sugar
  • 3 tsp of vanilla extract
  • 1 whole egg & 1 egg yolk
  • 300g chocolate chips (I recommend half white and half milk)

  • Regular oven: 180 degrees, gas mark 6
  • Cooking Time: 10 minutes
  • Servings:24 cookies

Method

  1. line 2 baking trays with greeseproof paper
  2. Preheat the oven
  3. Combine the flour and bicarbonate
  4. In a seperate (big) bowl, beat the sugar into the butter, until the mixture looks pale and smooth
  5. crack the whole egg into a glass, seperate the yolk from the second egg and add it to the first. Whisk together.
  6. Slowly add the eggs to the butter mix, stiring until well combined each time.
  7. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  8. Slowly fold the flour mixture into the egg mixture, until well combined.
  9. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  10. Portion out, 24 evenly sized cookies on to the 2 baking trays.
  11. Cook in the upper part of the oven for 10 minutes.

Notes:

  • You can make chocolate chips by dicing up chocolate bars (less money and often more tasty)
  • You can also add in different chocolates /sweets here, for instance rolo chunks, fudge, smarties etc
  • If you are super dedicated you could increase the heat of the oven to 200/gas mark 7 for the first 5 minutes and turn it down to gas mark 5/160 for the last 5 minutes (this way you will get super crispy but chewy cookies
  • You can use an ice cream scoop to help with cookie size when putting on the tray.
