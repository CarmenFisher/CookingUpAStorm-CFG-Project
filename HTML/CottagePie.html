Cottage pie

Ingredients

    --Filling--
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 large red onion/li>
  • 3 carrots
  • 50g frozen peas
  • 4 celery sticks
  • 6 mushrooms
  • 2 tsps dried thyme
  • 500g beef mince
  • 2 tbsp of Worcestershire Sauce
  • 500ml beef stock
  • 1tsbp cornflour to be mixed with a quarter of a cup of water, only when needed (if the sauce needs thickening)
    • --Mash--
  • 1kg potatoes, peeled and cut into quarters
  • 40g butter
  • 1tbsp milk
  • 50g grated cheese

  • Regular oven: 180 degrees, gas mark 6
  • Cooking Time: 30 minutes for hob, 30 minutes for oven
  • Servings:4

Method

  1. Preheat the oven
  2. Place the potatoes in a pan of boiling water. Cover and boil for 20 minutes. Turn off and drain the potatoes.
  3. In a seperate ovenproof casserole pan, fry off the onions on a low-medium heat, until translucent. Then add the mince, stiring continously, until brown and seprated.
  4. Add the thyme and remaining vegetables (except the frozen peas). Cook and stir for 5 minutes.
  5. Now add the stock, tomato puree and Worchester sauce. Stir together and heat for anothe r5 minutes
  6. If at this point the mixture still looks very wet, then makeup the cornflour mixture, turn the heat off and slowly pour the contexts into the mince, stirring quickly
    7. --Mash--
  7. Add the milk and butter to the potatoes. Pour over the milk
  8. Mash well with a masher
  9. Put the mash on the mincemeat and add some grated cheese on top
  10. Bake in the centre of the oven for 30 minutes or until the cheese is bubbling and browning.
  11. Dish out portion but allow to cool for 5 minutes before serving

Notes:

  • If you like super smooth mash you can use a handblender to mash the potatoes
  • Changing the meat to lamb will create Shepherds pie, try using rosemary instead of thyme.
  • Serve with spring greens to add to your 5 a day.
  • You can make this and leave it to keep warm in a slower cooker.
